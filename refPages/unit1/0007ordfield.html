Lesson 0007: Ordered Fields

Let $(F,+,\times)$ be a field and $(F,\preceq)$ a totally ordered set.
For any $a\in F$ we define the intervals $$ \begin{aligned} (a,\infty) &= \{ x\in F: a < x\} \\ [a,\infty) &= \{ x\in F: a\le x\} \end{aligned} $$ The interval $(a,\infty)$ is said to be open on the left and $[a,\infty)$ is closed on the left.
For $a,b\in F$, similar defintions apply for the intervals $$\begin{aligned} (-\infty,a) \\ (-\infty,a] \\ (-\infty,\infty)\\ (a,b) \\ (a,b] \\ [a,b) \\ [a,b] \end{aligned}$$ We define $F^\ast = F\cup\{-\infty,\infty\}$. If $a,b\in F$ then we say that $a$ is the left end-point of the interval $(a,b)$, and $b$ is the right end-point. Likewise for other intervals.
Every interval of the forms $(-\infty,\infty),(-\infty,a),(a,\infty),(a,b)$ is called an open interval.
Every interval of the forms $(-\infty,\infty),(-\infty,a],[a,\infty],[a,b]$ is called a closed interval.
If $X\subseteq F$ then we define the subset of positives, $$ X^+ = \{a\in X: 0\prec a \}$$ and the subset of negatives $$ X^- = \{a\in X: a\prec 0\} $$ and the subset of nonnegatives $$ X^{\preceq 0} = \{a\in X: 0\preceq a\} $$ and the subset of nonpositives $$ X^{\succeq 0} = \{a\in X: a\preceq 0\}$$
